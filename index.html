About

My interests sit where real-time graphics, computer vision, and AI integration meet. I like shipping a real artifact, pushing it for performance, and wiring in AI only where it genuinely helps.

Most recently I led Virtual Driving School, a webcam motion-controlled driving simulator that placed 2nd of 30 teams at the Giiku CAMP 2025 hackathon in Tokyo. At the BYU–Idaho University Store I build integration systems that thousands of students rely on every semester.

The skill I value most isn't the language I learned in class. It's how fast I can pick up the stack a problem actually needs. Three.js, MediaPipe, and the Gemini API weren't in any course; the build demanded them, so I learned them.

I'm especially drawn to problems where AI meets real trust-and-safety constraints, like deciding where the line should sit between what an AI system can touch and what it can't when you're handling confidential data. That's the kind of engineering I want to keep doing.
